\textbf{Problem 2} - Discrete curvature of a plane curve.

\textit{a)} Suppose we have a discrete curve given by $\{ \x_i \in \mathbb{R}^2 \}$ for $i = 1,\dots, n$.
Define an arc length functional $s(\x) : \mathbb{R}^{2n} \to \mathbb{R}_+$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
    s(\x) = \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \|\x_{i+1} - \x_{i}\|_2
\end{aligned}
$$

\textit{b)} Suppose $1< i < n$. Write an expression for $\nabla_{\x_i} s$ and show that
its norm is $2\sin \frac{\theta}{2}$, with $\theta$ being the turning angle.

$$
\begin{aligned}
    s(\x) &= \cdots + \|\x_i - \x_{i - 1}\|_2 + \| \x_{i+1} - \x_i \|_2 + \cdots\\
    \nabla_{\x_j} s(\x) 
    &= \frac{\x_{i} - \x_{i-1}}{\|\x_{i} - \x_{i-1}\|_2}
    -\frac{\x_{i+1} - \x_i}{\|\x_{i+1} - \x_i\|_2}\\
    &= \t_i - \t_{i+1}
\end{aligned}
$$

also,
$$
\begin{aligned}
    \|\nabla_{\x_j} s(\x)\|_2^2 &= (\t_i - \t_{i+1}) \cdot (\t_i - \t_{i+1}) \\ 
    &= \|\t_i\|_2^2 + \|\t_{i+1}\|_2^2 - 2\t_i\cdot\t_{i+1} \\
    &= 2 - 2\cos \theta_i\\
    &= 4 \sin^2 \frac{\theta_i}{2} 
\end{aligned}
$$
finally,
$$
    \|\nabla_{\x_j} s(\x)\|_2  = 2 \sin \frac{\theta_i}{2} 
$$

\textit{d)} Propose a measure of discrete per-vertex curvature of a 2D discrete curve.
$$
\begin{aligned}
    ds_\gamma[\v] &= \left< \w, \v \right>_{L_2} \\
    ds_{\x}[\x_j] &= \left< \nabla_{\x_j}s(\x), \x_j \right>_2 \\
    \| \w \|_2 &= \|-\kappa(\bar{s})N(\bar{s})\|_2 \\
    &= |\kappa(\bar{s})| \\
    &\approx \|\nabla_{\x_j} s(\x)\|_2 \\
    |\kappa| &\approx 2\sin \frac{\theta}{2}
\end{aligned}
$$
